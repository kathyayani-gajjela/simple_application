MicroBeads and MicroPLastics
Reduce Consumption Of MicroPlastics On Our Daily Life
Organic Clothes

Synthetic clothing is a huge contributor of microplastics.so choose eco-friendlier, sustainable clothing made from organic materials such as wool

Use wood or bamboo cutting boards

plastic cutting boards can be a significant source of microplastics in your diet, since repeated cutting on their surface can dislodge particles that adhere to food.

Reduce shellfish consumption

Microplastics that end up in the ocean are ingested by bottom-feeding shellfish. When you consume the shellfish, the microplastics are then ingested by you.
